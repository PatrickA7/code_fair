EVENTS

Business Innovation Challenge

This challenge, provided by the NT government, presents a real-life problems to students regarding how to communicate NT Road conditions using 21st century technologies. In 2016 Students had to create a prototype and or concept for solving the problem and then communicate this through a 5 minute presentation. Beside a monetary prize, the NT government have employed 5 students during summer as part of a development think tank so that their ideas can be turned into reality.

Coding Competitions

This competition is open to all CDU IT students (VET, and Higher Education). What could you enter? Apps; programs; mobile prototypes, websites, games that demonstrate your developing knowledge and any other innovative coding ideas you have that can be executed!

There are various categories associated with the level of study of students: novice, intermediate and advanced for both individuals and groups. There is also an overall innovation prize awarded each year.

Poster Competition

This competition is open to all CDU IT students (VET, and Higher Education). The poster competition aims to encourage students to communicate their IT work and develop and expand their visual communication skills.

What could you enter? Make a poster about what interests you regarding information technology it's as simple as that. Interested in apps or a certain app? Make a poster, interested in artifical intelligence? Make a poster. Interested in how technology effects everyday life? Make a poster! and Submit!

Code Hour Workshops

In 2016 four, one hour workshops were run for 45 students from 3 local high schools (Casuarina Senior College, Darwin High School and Essington) and university staff.This event was very popular with the school students and university staff all learning many new interesting things

That is why we have the Code Hour Workshops back again this year!!!

Industry Employer Speed Dating

Don't know how to create you resume? Want to network within the industry or simply want to come along for the ride? Employer Speed provides graduating students an opportunity to meet local employers.

In 2016 eighteen students participated in this event. They gave one minute interviews to prosepective employeers and were given feedback on appropriate dress; body language; oral presentation skills and CV preparations.

Academic Awards

To encourage a greater emphasis on academic endeavours, last year we introduced a new catergory to recognise students who achieve highly in each subject taught in IT. Students who receive this award earn the highest scores in each unit.

Last year, 9 students were recognise for their academic endeavours. Hopefully this year will have even more! So put the effort into your study and you can could rewarded for it!
